Why this is real science: no single answer decides anything. In the live project, each light curve is shown to many people; the crowd's agreement — checked against hidden known-answer cards like these — is what flags a candidate for a professional to follow up. We also hide faint synthetic planets in the data (an injection–recovery test) to measure exactly what fraction the crowd catches. That's how we know it works, in numbers.
