The Search — Data Lab

You're shown a light curve — a star's brightness measured over time by NASA's TESS planet-hunting telescope. A planet crossing its star makes a tiny, repeating dip. Your job: tell a real planet from noise, a glitch, or two stars eclipsing each other. Real cards use real TESS data and each has a known answer, so your accuracy is measured for real.

🎯 Mono-Transit Hunt — hunt the planets that cross their star just once. Automated pipelines search for repeating dips, so they throw single transits away — but human eyes catch them, and they're the #1 thing citizen scientists find. Spot the lone dip, or call it noise.
